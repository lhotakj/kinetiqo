How is VO₂max computed?

1. Storer-Davis equation

Each qualifying ride's best 5-minute average power (MAP) is converted to a VO₂max estimate:

VO₂max = (10.8 × MAP / weight) + 7

Storer, T.W., Davis, J.A., & Caiozzo, V.J. (1990). Medicine and Science in Sports and Exercise, 22(5), 704-712.

2. Qualifying ride filters

Not every ride is used — only efforts that represent genuine fitness are kept, similar to Garmin/Firstbeat:

  • Minimum duration — at least 20 minutes of power data.
  • Best per day — if you ride twice on the same day, only the highest VO₂max counts.
  • Outlier rejection — rides below 75 % of the median are excluded (recovery / easy rides).

3. Headline value

The large number shown above is the best raw VO₂max from the single ride with the highest 5-minute power across all your cycling activities.

4. Chart trend line (Firstbeat-style)

The chart shows two layers:

  • Faded dots — raw per-ride VO₂max (after filtering).
  • Solid line — an asymmetric Exponentially Weighted Moving Average (EWMA):
  • ↗ Rise α = 0.3 — improvements are absorbed in ~3 rides.
  • ↘ Fall α = 0.07 — a single bad ride barely moves the line.
  • ⏸ Daily decay = 0.1 % — inactivity slowly reduces the estimate (~3 % after 30 idle days).

Inspired by: Firstbeat Technologies (2014). VO₂max Estimation — White Paper.
